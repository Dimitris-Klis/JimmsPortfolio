Boat Duty:
Boat Duty was made during the 2nd year of Middle School, for a Pancypriot competition called "Logipaignio" and "Game Development Challenge", which lasted for about half a year. My computer science teacher was the one who found the competition. This competition was split into 3 age groups: Middle School Students, High School Students & University Students.

Due to the competition requiring a team, I was assigned with 4 other students from my school. The theme was very vague: water. We all quickly came up with the idea of a boat that rides around a polluted lake. Your goal was to use the trash in said lake to make 3 things: A Radio Tower, a TV Station & some letters.

I was the person who did most of the work, since the others didn't know much about programming or music production. However, one of three teammates was extremely helpful during the final day before the submission of the project. He made the final 3D models for the game. I managed to make my first inventory system with this game, although it's not the best.

Despite a lot of procrastination, and a couple of serious bugs, we managed to make it to Phase 2. We quickly found out why: The majority of the contestants that made it to phase 2 made their games in scratch (They could be made in less than a week). In fact, our game could compete with the other age groups.

Unfortunately, due to age discrimination, we were placed third, right under another bad scratch game. I have no idea what has happened to this yearly competition, because it's been gone since its end in 2022.

itch.io: Boat Duty!
A Roll Of Luck:
This game is a submission for the Game Maker's Toolkit Game Jam 2022.

Originally meant to be a board game, that idea was quickly scrapped during the final day, and it quickly became a top down shooter. The unique mechanic here is: every time you collect ammo, you get a random gun.

A Roll Of Luck was submitted 14 minutes before the end of the jam. The menu was made 1 or 2 weeks ago to save time. The music was made by me months ago and I repurposed it for the game.

itch.io: A Roll Of Luck
Quac's Bakefull Delivery:
One of the most simple but fun games I've made. A submission for the Game Maker's Toolkit Game Jam of 2023. You play as a duck, who has a bakery and gives bread to people.

This is a pretty good interpretation of the theme "Roles Reversed". I've made the music, most of the sounds and the graphics, like always.

My biggest mistake was using the "Long Term Support" Unity version 2022.3.4f1. This version has a very weird bug when the game is built: The canvas will be bigger than your screen and to fix this, you need to reload the scene.

This bug alone has caused me great pain and because of a few other bugs, I will be using 2021.3.6f1 for future projects.

itch.io: Quac's Bakefull Delivery

Mini Golf Dungeon:
A pretty fun and polished game. I don't remember when I had the idea of making it. Was finished for a long time but I only uploaded it to itch.io after the release of "Boat Duty!".

The level is procedurally generated. Different rooms & different obstacles every single time. There is no end goal, apart from getting the golf ball in the hole.

There are also time based & stroke based scores. There is one bug in this game: The minimap displays blocked off entries to rooms.

itch.io: Mini Golf Dungeon
School Timetable:
The School Timetable Program began development during the final year of Middle School.

At first, there was only an android version that existed only on my phone. Its features included: making timetables, editing their cells, saving, loading and copying/pasting boards. It also displayed in how much time the next lesson or break would begin.

School start times, lesson lengths & break lengths were all hardcoded. The only language was Greek.

During the second semester, more features were added, like keeping track of lost lessons and note writing.

This program has been finalized, localized and uploaded to itch.io for Windows & Android during the beggining of the first year of High School.

itch.io: School Timetable
